About

This unripe plantain meal is not like the commonly known unripe plantain porridge with vegetables. This is a different version originated from my mum's kitchen. While I may not have images of it (because I was more concerned in finishing my portion whenever I get the opportunity to eat this delicacy), I will do my best to describe it very well so you will have a picture of it in your head. I will then share other images that looks like it.

This particular unripe platain meal involves boiling unripe plaitain till it is cooked, the boiled unripe plantain is then diced into small cubes. the vegetable is sliced and cooked with a little oil and other indredients then the diced plantain is poured in a different pot and the vegetable added and stirred. Almost like a stir-fry but not a stir fry...
Sorry, I know you are finding it hard to understand but that is how delicious the meal is too!

Let's dive right in!

Ingredients

Ingredients Measurement Image
Unripe plantains 4 medium-sizes
Pumpkin leaves Any desired quantity
Onion 1 Large size
Fresh pepper 4-6 bonnet
Palm Oil 2 Cooking Spoonful
Cube seasoning 2 cubes
Salt Quantity to taste
Protein (Preferably smoked fish) Any desired quantity
African Oil bean seed(Ukpaka) Any desired quantity

Procedure

  1. Wash your plantains, trim both edges, peel off the skin and cut into small cubes. Pour into a sizeable pot and cook (with small quantity of salt just to taste) until it is well cooked. Bring it down, sieve the water and cover the plantain in the pot.
  2. Wash the smoked fish thoroughly with salt and keep aside.
  3. Wash your pumpkin leaves and finely slice it and set aside
  4. Add the oil in an empty pot and put on fire, add the magie cubes, add your sliced onions and pepper and also add the smoked fish. Cook for about 2 minutes.
  5. Add the leaves and stir for about 2 minutes (Vegetables are cooked for very little time)
  6. Finally pour the unripe plantain you boiled earlier, add the ukpaka and stir.
  7. Your food is ready for munch!

Serve with your favourite, healthy drink! (I would recommend tigernut milk)
